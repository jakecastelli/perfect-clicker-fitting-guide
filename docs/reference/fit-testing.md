# Fit Testing Reference

Use this page as the troubleshooting map when a clicker does not feel right.

## Common Symptoms

| Symptom | Likely cause | Next test |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Parts will not assemble | Clearance too small, elephant foot, over-extrusion, or rough seam placement. | Measure mating faces, inspect first layer, try compensation on the affected axis. |
| Moving area is fused | First layer squish, brim/support contact, or insufficient clearance. | Inspect layer preview and retest with first-layer or compensation changes. |
| Click is weak or mushy | Flexible area too thin, wall layout too soft, material too flexible, or layer direction is wrong. | Compare wall count, orientation, and filament stiffness. |
| Clicker rattles | Clearance too large or printed walls undersized. | Measure printed features and reduce clearance or compensation. |
| One side rubs | Warping, uneven cooling, seam blob, or asymmetric geometry. | Rotate the model, inspect seam position, and compare left/right dimensions. |
| Keychain loop breaks | Layer direction or wall count is weak for the load. | Change orientation, add walls, or document the safe-use limit. |

## Measurement Notes

Record measurements with calipers when possible:

- Designed outside dimension.
- Printed outside dimension.
- Designed hole or socket size.
- Printed hole or socket size.
- Actual clearance between moving parts.
- Whether the issue is global or local.

## Fit Language

Use consistent terms in the guide:

- **Fused:** parts that should move are bonded by the print.
- **Tight:** parts move or assemble only with force.
- **Snug:** parts assemble with light pressure and stay seated.
- **Free:** parts move without binding.
- **Loose:** parts rattle, wobble, or detach too easily.
